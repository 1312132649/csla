Brian Criswell replied on Wednesday, December 06, 2006

  • It focuses that developer toward making maintainable, object -oriented code.
  • It provides solutions for many of the plumbing issues that every application has, allowing the business object developer to focus on the behavior of the business objects.
  • It integrates with many other parts of the .NET framework so that developers do not have to reinvent the wheel with issues such as security and UI presentation of data.
  • It has a worldwide community that will help new and experienced users with questions that they have.
  • It keeps pace with new technologies and incorporates those that are applicable.
  • It has a generous license that allows developers to modify their own copies of the framework.
  • Features that are not used do not cause a performance/memory hit.
