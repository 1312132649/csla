RockfordLhotka replied on Thursday, May 18, 2006

This is a contentious issue.

Several people have proposed replacing General Discussion with separate groups for version 1.x and 2.x.

At least the same number of people think that it is best to keep the discussions all in a single group, because there's often quite a lot of overlap between them.

My fear is that important discussions, like ones around OO design or data access issues, will end up in a version-specific forum, when those are universal issues that have little or nothing to do with "version 1" or "version 2".

Which means at least 3 forums - General, V1 and V2. But more importantly, it means people having the discipline to only put version-specific technical threads in V1/V2 - and I very much doubt that would happen.

It is also the case that I have a limited number of email addresses and so breaking into too many forums means some won't have email (like this admin one), and will be web-only.
