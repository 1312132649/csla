RockfordLhotka replied on Tuesday, May 30, 2006

Glenn, though I know this is resolved, I want to reply for future reference.

It appears that the email gateway used by Community Server 2.0 can run into issues where it out and out crashes. The gateway is a Windows Service that listens on an MSMQ queue and relays the messages from that queue through SMTP to you. If it crashes, then no email goes out (though it does stay in the queue) until the service is restarted.

At the moment there doesn't appear to be any management or monitoring capability around the gateway other than logging into the server as an administrator (which I obviously can't allow others to do), so the only way to restart the service is to have me log in and restart it.

Over the Memorial Day holiday weekend I was out of town on a family camping trip, and Murphy's Law dictated that the thing crashed    I restarted it when I got back home.

Hopefully the mail gateway software will get an upgrade and/or CS 2.0 will get some manangement tools for the gateway so I can grant web access to other people so the odds of catching/fixing the issue improves. Until then, we'll just have to live with it. Regardless, this (I think) remains far superior to MSN
