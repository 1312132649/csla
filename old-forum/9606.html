RockfordLhotka replied on Wednesday, October 06, 2010

It would be nice in some ways, that's true. As with most things this is more complex than it might first appear.

Things like code camps aren't so bad, but I usually get paid to speak, and so on one hand don't want to give away for free what otherwise pays the bills. Also when a conference pays me to speak they don't want me to give the content away for free either (usually).

But code camps are a community-building effort. Donated time and effort to make the community stronger - and of course to market my paid content and Magenic's services. I'd love to be completely altruistic, but that's not the world we live in.

My code camp demos are, or will be, in the CSLA 4 Samples download. I don't plan to have many (any?) slides, since I can easily fill the time walking through the demos. So I think it is safe to say that the CSLA community will have full access to the assets from the presentations via the Samples download.

Specifically the Samples\NET\cs\SimpleNTier remains simple, but now includes numerous types of interface (in the 4.1 release), including a Silverlight UI, and (by this weekend) an ASP.NET MVC UI.
