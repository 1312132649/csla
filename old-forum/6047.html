RockfordLhotka posted on Tuesday, December 23, 2008

Many of you may have noticed that www.lhotka.net has been very sluggish for the past several days.

I have discovered that troubleshooting performance issues on a web server is a real challenge. Especially when I haven't changed anything in a long time, and so had no clues about where to look.

IIS has terrible (no?) tools for figuring out which virtual directory or page is consuming all your CPU. And that's exactly what was happening - IIS was consuming 100% of the CPU constantly.

So I did what I could, and eventually started turning off anonymous access to each vdir, one at a time. Eventually I turned off the right one, and the CPU dropped to normal levels. It was the wiki.

Now I have looked through the wiki logs, and discovered that it was not used hardly at all by the community, but in the past week it seems that some spammer wrote a script to flood it with spam. And it seems that flexwiki (the software I was using) gets insanely slow if a single page has a bajillion edits. And this evil person's script did tend to pick on just 2-3 pages to edit over and over and over and...

So the wiki is offline, probably permanently. As a tool it never became very useful, and as a time-sink for me it is simply not worth the effort.
